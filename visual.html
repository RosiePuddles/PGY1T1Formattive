CO2 by population size

To illustrate a common misconception about certain countries emitting more CO2 than others, specifically Russia and China, the map below shows each country's share of global carbon emissions as a proportion of it's share of global population. Ideally, this number would be 1, when a country emits the same proportion of global CO2 as it has people. Of all thr countries included, the closest to having a score of 1 was Tanzania with 0.928. Out of interest, the lowest scoring country was Burundi at 0.144.
The scale for this choropleth is the greener the colour, the better; and the pinker, the worse.
